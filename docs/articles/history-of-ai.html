History of AI

From symbolic systems to deep learning.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has evolved through several eras, including symbolic AI, expert systems, statistical learning, and modern deep learning driven by large datasets and GPUs.

Early ideas

The Dartmouth conference (1956) is often cited as the symbolic beginning of AI as a field, focusing on logic, search, and symbolic manipulation.

Expert systems and winters

In the 1970s–80s, expert systems saw commercial interest but also highlighted limitations, contributing to cycles known as AI winters when funding and enthusiasm declined.

Modern deep learning

Advances in compute, data availability, and techniques such as backpropagation, CNNs, and transformers led to breakthroughs in vision, speech, and language.
